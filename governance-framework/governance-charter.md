## 1. Purpose

This Cybersecurity Governance Charter ("Charter") establishes the formal authority, structure, scope, principles, and operational guidelines for Acme Cloud Services' enterprise cybersecurity governance program. It defines the governance model through which cybersecurity risk is identified, assessed, managed, and reported across the organization.

Cybersecurity governance is the system of policies, processes, accountability structures, and oversight mechanisms through which the Board of Directors and executive leadership ensure that cybersecurity strategy is aligned with business objectives, resources are appropriately directed, and risks are managed within the organization's defined risk appetite.

This Charter authorizes the establishment of the **Cybersecurity Governance Committee (CGC)** and its subordinate working groups, defines their authority and responsibilities, and sets the expectations for governance program operation.

## 3. Governance Principles

The cybersecurity governance program at Acme Cloud Services is founded on the following principles:

**3.1 Accountability**
Cybersecurity risk ownership is assigned to named individuals at the business unit level. Every risk, control, and policy has a designated owner who is responsible for its management and is accountable for outcomes within their domain.

**3.2 Risk-Based Approach**
Governance decisions, control investments, and remediation priorities are driven by formal risk assessment using documented methodology. Resources are directed toward the risks that pose the greatest potential harm to the organization, its customers, and its data.

**3.3 Alignment with Business Objectives**
The governance program exists to enable the business, not obstruct it. Cybersecurity controls are designed to protect business value while minimizing friction for legitimate business activities. Security investments are justified through business impact analysis.

**3.4 Continuous Improvement**
The governance program is subject to regular review, assessment, and enhancement. Control effectiveness is measured, gaps are identified, and the program evolves in response to changes in the threat landscape, business environment, and regulatory requirements.

**3.5 Transparency and Communication**
Risk posture, compliance status, and governance activities are communicated regularly and honestly to all relevant stakeholders, including the Board of Directors, executive leadership, and customers where contractually required.

**3.6 Least Privilege and Need-to-Know**
Access to systems, data, and information is granted on the basis of business need. Excessive privilege is a governance failure, not a technical inconvenience.

**3.7 Defense in Depth**
No single control is assumed to be sufficient. The governance program mandates layered controls across people, process, and technology dimensions.

**3.8 Shared Responsibility**
Cybersecurity is a shared responsibility across the organization. The governance program establishes clear ownership while recognizing that every employee plays a role in protecting the organization.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Board of Directors

The Board of Directors holds ultimate accountability for cybersecurity governance at Acme Cloud Services. The Board:

- Approves the Cybersecurity Governance Charter and all subsequent revisions
- Reviews and accepts the organization's cybersecurity risk appetite statement annually
- Receives quarterly cybersecurity risk briefings from the CISO
- Holds executive leadership accountable for cybersecurity program outcomes
- Approves major cybersecurity investments above the CISO's spending authority
- Ensures cybersecurity risk is considered in all material business decisions

### 4.2 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

The CEO:
- Champions cybersecurity as a business priority at the executive level
- Allocates budget and resources consistent with the approved cybersecurity strategy
- Ensures cybersecurity considerations are embedded in business strategy and M&A activities
- Holds the CISO accountable for program performance

### 4.3 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

The CISO holds executive responsibility for the cybersecurity governance program. The CISO:

- Owns this Charter and is responsible for its maintenance and enforcement
- Chairs the Cybersecurity Governance Committee
- Develops and maintains the enterprise cybersecurity strategy
- Reports risk posture to the Board quarterly and to the executive team monthly
- Approves all security policies and standards
- Has final authority on security exceptions and risk acceptance decisions above $500K potential impact
- Serves as the primary liaison between the cybersecurity function and the Board's Audit Committee

### 4.4 Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

The CTO:
- Co-owns the technology risk domain with the CISO
- Ensures security requirements are embedded in system design and engineering processes
- Approves technology architecture decisions with security implications
- Owns remediation of technical vulnerabilities within engineering systems

### 4.5 Chief Risk Officer (CRO) / VP of Legal & Compliance

The CRO/VP Legal:
- Owns the enterprise risk management framework in coordination with the CISO
- Ensures cybersecurity risk is reflected in the enterprise risk register
- Manages regulatory compliance obligations (SOC 2, GDPR, CCPA, etc.)
- Owns vendor contract review for security requirements
- Advises on risk acceptance decisions with legal or regulatory implications

### 4.6 Business Unit Leaders

Business Unit Leaders (including VP Engineering, VP Product, VP Sales, VP Finance):
- Serve as risk owners for risks within their domain
- Ensure security policies are adhered to within their teams
- Approve risk acceptance within their authority threshold
- Participate in the Cybersecurity Governance Committee as required
- Escalate security events and potential incidents immediately

### 4.7 IT Operations / Infrastructure Team

The IT Operations team:
- Implements and maintains technical controls as directed by the governance program
- Manages vulnerability remediation within assigned SLAs
- Provides evidence for control assessments and audits
- Maintains system access controls and reviews as scheduled

### 4.8 All Employees and Contractors

All individuals with access to Acme Cloud Services systems:
- Complete mandatory security awareness training annually (and upon onboarding)
- Adhere to all security policies, standards, and procedures
- Report security incidents, policy violations, and suspicious activity
- Participate in security exercises and simulated phishing campaigns

## 5. Cybersecurity Governance Committee (CGC)

### 5.1 Establishment

The Cybersecurity Governance Committee is hereby established under the authority of this Charter. The CGC is the primary decision-making body for cybersecurity governance matters below the Board level.

### 5.2 Membership

| Role | Member | Voting? |
|---|---|---|
| Chair | Chief Information Security Officer | Yes |
| Co-Chair | Chief Technology Officer | Yes |
| Member | Chief Risk Officer / VP Legal | Yes |
| Member | VP Engineering | Yes |
| Member | VP Product | Yes |
| Member | VP Finance | Yes |
| Member | VP Human Resources | Yes |
| Member | VP Sales (when applicable) | Yes |
| Observer | Internal Audit (if applicable) | No |
| Observer | External Auditors (as invited) | No |

Quorum requires the presence of the Chair plus a minimum of four voting members.

### 5.3 CGC Responsibilities

The CGC:
- Reviews and approves security policies and standards
- Reviews the enterprise risk register and risk treatment plans quarterly
- Approves risk acceptance decisions within its authority level (up to $2M potential impact)
- Reviews control assessment results and remediation status
- Monitors compliance with SOC 2, NIST CSF, and other applicable frameworks
- Reviews and approves exceptions to security policies
- Escalates material risks to the Board Audit Committee as appropriate

### 5.4 Meeting Cadence

| Meeting Type | Frequency | Participants | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| CGC Quarterly Review | Quarterly | Full CGC | Risk register review, compliance status, remediation update |
| Monthly Risk Briefing | Monthly | CISO + risk owners | Risk trending, open items, escalations |
| Incident Review | As needed | CISO + relevant parties | Post-incident governance actions |
| Annual Program Review | Annually | Full CGC + Board rep | Program effectiveness, strategy update, budget review |

### 5.5 Working Groups

The CGC authorizes the following standing working groups:

**Identity and Access Management Working Group**
- Chair: IT Operations Manager
- Focus: IAM controls, privileged access, user lifecycle management

**Cloud Security Working Group**
- Chair: Lead Cloud Architect
- Focus: Azure and AWS security posture, cloud configuration standards

**Vendor Risk Working Group**
- Chair: VP Legal / Procurement Lead
- Focus: Vendor security assessments, third-party risk management

**Incident Response Working Group**
- Chair: Security Operations Lead
- Focus: IR readiness, tabletop exercises, incident playbooks

## 6. Risk Escalation Process

### 6.1 Escalation Thresholds

| Risk Level | Potential Impact | Authority | Escalation Path |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low | < $100K | Risk Owner | Document and treat; report to CISO quarterly |
| Medium | $100K – $500K | CISO | Document, treat, and report to CGC |
| High | $500K – $2M | CGC | Treat with urgency; report to Board Audit Committee |
| Critical | > $2M | Board | Immediate Board notification; may require public disclosure |

### 6.2 Incident Escalation

Security incidents with confirmed or suspected material impact must be escalated to the CISO within one (1) hour of identification. The CISO determines whether Board notification, regulatory disclosure, or customer notification is required within 24 hours.

### 6.3 Exception Escalation

Policy exceptions are submitted via the formal Exception Request process. Exceptions that persist beyond 90 days, affect more than 100 users/systems, or involve High/Critical risk must be approved by the CGC. Long-term exceptions are reviewed at each quarterly CGC meeting.

## 8. Reporting Requirements

### 8.1 Board Reporting (Quarterly)

The CISO provides a quarterly cybersecurity risk briefing to the Board containing:
- Enterprise risk posture summary
- Top 10 risks and trends
- Compliance status (SOC 2, regulatory)
- Open High/Critical findings
- Security incident summary
- Strategic program updates

### 8.2 Executive Reporting (Monthly)

The CISO provides a monthly risk briefing to the executive team containing:
- Risk register changes
- Remediation status updates
- Control assessment results
- KPI/KRI dashboard
- Upcoming compliance milestones

### 8.3 Operational Reporting (Ongoing)

The Security Operations team provides:
- Weekly vulnerability and patch status
- Phishing simulation results monthly
- Security awareness training completion status
- Incident and near-miss reports as they occur
